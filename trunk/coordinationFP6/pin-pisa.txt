A. Gestione della conoscenza matematica - conoscenza concreta, giornali attivi


Nel campo computazionale numerico gli strumenti software di base (Fortran, C, biblioteche numeriche standard) sono un fatto acquisito e ben consolidato, ed esistono giornali anche elettronici che raccolgono i contributi software a carattere originale; inoltre la distinzione tra "ricerca analitica pura" e "analisi numerica" %/1€Œiso8859-15è molto forte, ed i due settori sono ben distinti.

La situazione %/1€Œiso8859-15è molto pi%/1€Œiso8859-15ù complessa nel settore dell'algebra, dove la distinzione tra ricerca astratta e aspetti computazionali %/1€Œiso8859-15è molto pi%/1€Œiso8859-15ù sfumata, come esemplificato anche dal presente progetto nazionale. Inoltre il software tende ad essere estremamente pi%/1€Œiso8859-15ù complesso, ed anzicch%/1€Œiso8859-15é essere basato su linguaggi standardizzati e librerie, tende ad organizzarsi tramite sistemi di calcolo algebrico pi%/1€Œiso8859-15ù complessi e spesso interattivi. Tali sistemi tendono ad avere sintassi incompatibile, ed %/1€Œiso8859-15è quindi molto difficile far dialogare sistemi differenti, che spesso si basano su software proprietario e "chiuso".

In tale contesto, spesso gli articoli scientifici di algebra computazionale descrivono algoritmi in maniera schematica, facendo riferimento a loro implementazioni "sperimentali" dichiarate "disponibili" ma spesso aleatorie, e non mantenute. In tal modo il lavoro di implementazione non %/1€Œiso8859-15è verificabile, ed %/1€Œiso8859-15è spesso inutilizzabile.

Recentemente si %/1€Œiso8859-15è verificata una significativa novit%/1€Œiso8859-15à, colla disponibilit%/1€Œiso8859-15à di software "libero" (v. http://www.gnu.org per una definizione di software "libero") affidabile ed efficiente; fino a pochi anni fa questa possibilit%/1€Œiso8859-15à era limitato a sistemi specializzati, ad esempio in algebra commutativa o teoria dei gruppi (pionieri sono stati Macaulay e GAP), di recente c'%/1€Œiso8859-15è stato un rilancio di Maxima, ed il passaggio di AXIOM ad una licenza "libera". In tale modo, anche col passaggio di Singular ed il previsto passaggio di CoCoA ad una licenza "libera" nel campo dell'algebra, in particolare di quella commutativa, ci sar%/1€Œiso8859-15à ampia disponibilit%/1€Œiso8859-15à di software libero.

Inoltre ricerche recenti, anche strutturate in progetti europei ed internazionali, cercano di far interagire sistemi eterogenei tramite un protocollo di scambio di dati, e di gestione delle conoscenze matematiche (progetti OpenMath, MONET e MOWGLI).

In questo contesto si pone il problema di utilizzare tali strumenti per rimediare alle difficolt%/1€Œiso8859-15à di accesso agli algoritmi algebrici presentati nella letteratura scientifica, garantendo un ambiente in cui questi possano essere facilmente implementati, verificati ed utilizzati. Lo strumento che vogliamo indagare in tale contesto %/1€Œiso8859-15è quello di un "giornale attivo". Vedi la descrizione nella sezione del programma.



B. Metodi computazionali in algebra commutativa e geometria algebrica


Le basi di Gr%/1€Œiso8859-15öbner sono lo strumento principale per lo studio di algoritmi in algebra commutativa, geometria algebrica e geometria algebrica reale. Accompagnati dalla fattorizzazione e dall'algebra lineare (e, per la geometria algebrica reale, da calcoli di segnatura) sono la base di moltissimi algoritmi di "alto livello" in geometria algebrica.

I proponenti hanno una consolidata esperienza in tale campo, ed intendono continuare ad occuparsi di tali temi.




B2. Geometria algebrica reale

La geometria algebrica reale algoritmica si differenzia dalla geometria algebrica complessa in quanto le propriet%/1€Œiso8859-15à topologiche non sono conseguenza delle propriet%/1€Œiso8859-15à algebriche, ma richiedono strumenti appositi.

Uno strumento classico di indagine %/1€Œiso8859-15è la decomposizione cilindrica algebrica, (CAD), che per%/1€Œiso8859-15ò %/1€Œiso8859-15è piuttosto uno strumento di interesse teorico per la sua grande complessit%/1€Œiso8859-15à sia teorica che pratica.

Recentemente lo studio di algoritmi che, pur non migliorando la complessit%/1€Œiso8859-15à teorica asintotica, rendano possibili calcoli significativi ha fatto notevoli progressi.



C. Algoritmi di base


Il progresso nel campo dell'algebra commutativa computazionale si svolge secondo due linee: sviluppo di nuovi algoritmi ad alto livello, e miglioramento degli algoritmi di base. Quest'ultimo richiede anche implementazioni efficaci e sperimentazioni accurate.

Recentemente si sono avuti sviluppi interessanti colla messa a punto di nuovi algoritmi o la specifica e l'implementazione accurata di algoritmi noti. In particolare l'uso di aritmetiche numeriche e ibride negli algoritmi algebrici ha avuto notevoli progressi.



D. Applicazioni

Le applicazioni dei metodi di algebra computazionale sono innumerevoli; il nostro piano di lavoro prevede di concenrarsi sui temi dei codici e del Computer Aided Geometric Design (CAGD).

Nel campo dei codici correttori, lo studio della variet%/1€Œiso8859-15à delle sindromi con tecniche di basi di gr%/1€Œiso8859-15öbner ha permesso di ottenere risultati importanti sulla distanza di codici BCH.

Recentemente i miglioramenti agli algoritmi di calcolo di basi di Gr%/1€Œiso8859-15öbner hanno permesso di attaccare con successo una famiglia di codici crittografici che resisteva da anni; contemporaneamente, alcuni codici crittografici standard (compreso il recentissimo AES) hanno poi dimostrato di avere una possibilit%/1€Œiso8859-15à di attacco tramite la risoluzione di sistemi polinomiali. D'altra parte, nuove tecniche algebriche sono state proposte per la costruzione di nuovi codici crittografici.

Nel campo del CAGD i problemi di parametrizzazione, implicitizzazione e descrizione di propriet%/1€Œiso8859-15à topologiche hanno avuto un ruolo centrale.

The four themes will have equal weight in the project, but in the description we will give more space to the point A, that is newer hence requires a more detailed description.


A Mathematical knowledge management - Concrete knowledge, active journals.

In the numerical computational domain, the basic software tools (Fortran, C, numerical libraries) are well established, and journals (electronic or not) exist that collect original software contributions. Moreover, the distinction between pure research in mathematical analysis and numerical analysis is well established, in two distinct scientific domains.

In algebra, the situation is much different; the distinction between abstract research and computational aspects is much lighter, and is becomming more and more blurred: our project being an example thereof. Moreover, the software is considerably more complex, and is not based on standard languages and libraries, but is mainly organized in interactive computer algebra systems.

The situation is complicated since these systems have incompatible syntax, and it is difficult to make such systems interact (they are moreover often closed and proprietary).

In this context, often the papers in computer algebra describe the algorithms in a schematic way, referring to "experimental" implementations declared "available" but often harly available, difficult to compile, and not maintained. In this way, the implementation work is not verifiable, and often useless.

Recently, a significant shift has been verified, with the availability of "free' software (see http://www.gnu.org for a definition) reliable and efficient; while some years ago this was limited to specialized systems in commutative algebra or grout theory (procursors have been Macaulay and GAP) recenty there has been a new life for Maxima, and Axiom is now available with a free licence. With the new licencing policy for Singular, and the anticipated new release of CoCoA, there will be wide availability of free software at least in commutative algebra.

Recent research, structured also in european and international projects, makes interaction between heterogeneous systems possible, through an exchange protocol for mathematical data and the handling of mathematical knowledge (projects OpenMath, MONET, MOWGLI)

In this context we pose the problem of use these tools to overcome the difficulty of accessing the algebraic algorithms present in the scientific literature, providing an environment in which these can be implemented, verified documented and used. The tool that we want to investigate is an "active journal". See the description in the programme section.



B1: Computational methods in commutative algebra and algebraic geometry.

Groebner bases are the main tool for the algorithms in commutative algebra, algebraic geometry and real algebraic geometry. Together with factorization and linear algebra (and, for real geometry, signature computations) they are the basis of most "high level" algorithms in algebraic geometry.

The proposers have a consolidated experience in this domain, and will continue to develop these themes.



B2 Real algebraic geometry

Algorithmic real algebraic geometry is different from complex algebraic geometry since the topological properties are not the consequence of algebraic properties, but require special tools.

A classical investigation tool is the Cylindrical Algebraic decomposition (CAD), that is rather of thoeretical interest because of its high complexity, both theoretical and practical.

Recently, the study of algorithms that, although not improving the asymptotic complexity, allow significant computations, has had striking progresses.


C: basic algorithms

Progress in computational commutative algebra follows two lines: development of new high-level algorithms, and improvements in basic algorithms. This last requires careful implementations and accurate experiments.

Recently progresses have been made with the specification and the tuning of new algorithms and the careful implementation of old ones. In particular the use of numerical or hybrid arithmetics has seen considerable progress.


D: Applications

There are many applications of computational commutative algebra. Our workplan concentrates on coding and Computer Aided Geometric Design (CAGD).

In error correcting codes, the study of syndrom varieties with Groebner basis techniques has allowed to obtain important results on the distance of BCH codes.

Recently the improvements in Groebner basis computation has allowed to successfully attack a family of cryptographic codes that resisted since several years; at the same time, some standard cryptosystems (including the very recent standard AES) have been discovered to be subject to attacks using the solution of polynomial systems. New algebraic techniques have been proposed to construct new cryptosystems.

In CAGD the problems of parametrization, implicitization and the description of topological properties have a central role.

Definiamo "giornale attivo" un giornale scientifico elettronico a cui soggiaccia un insieme di software che combini strumenti di tipografia e visualizzazione scientifica (TeX, LaTeX, MathML, ecc.) e strumenti computazionali tali da permettere ad un articolo di carattere algoritmico sia di essere stampato, che di essere "eseguito".

Per la caratteristica stessa della ricerca scientifica %/1€Œiso8859-15è essenziale che tutto l'ambiente del "giornale attivo", compreso il sistema operativo, sia "libero", onde permettere la distribuzione e l'esame scientifico senza vincoli di licenza o di segretezza.

Sar%/1€Œiso8859-15à inoltre prevista la possibilit%/1€Œiso8859-15à di sottoporre al giornale del software per espandere l'infrastruttura di base; tale software sar%/1€Œiso8859-15à inserito nel sistema in seguito a "peer review", e sulla base di un impegno di disponibilit%/1€Œiso8859-15à libera e piani di manutenzione.

Il lavoro proposto nell'ambito delprogetto di ricerca consiste in diverse parti, complessivamente consistenti in uno studio di fattibilit%/1€Œiso8859-15à:

A. Mathematical knowledge management - Concrete knowledge, active journals.

We define an "Active journal" a scientific electronic journal with an
underlying software system combining a scientific rendering engine
(TeX, LaTeX, MathML, etc) and computing tools that allow not only to
read and print a scientific paper, but also to "execute" it.

An essential component of such an "active journal" is the possibility
of inserting theoretical results of effective type, to be used as a
basis of knowledge as input of deduction methods, as well as practical
results (like sets of examples on which one can compute or conjecture
properties); it is also important that the whole environment is
"free", to allow redistribution and scientific scrutiny without
licence or secrecy limits, as is the true character of scientific
research.

It will be possible to submit to the journal further basic software,
to extend the journal's infrastructure. the contributed software will
be inserted in the journal infrastructure after a "peer review", on
the basis of free availability and maintenance committments.

The journal will be available with network access, with a limited
availability of resources, in a main site and several mirrors, and
will be moreover distributed as a complete system, comprehensive of
operating system, basic software and scientific contributions, to
allow the installation on the user's machines.

The work performed towards this aim in the proposed project willy
consist in different parts, constituting a "proof of concept":

1 - identify the necessary or expected characteristics;

2 - preparation of a prototype;

3 - test of the prototype, with the cooperations of users, both
"active" (potential contributors) or "passive" (researchers interested
in the scientific contributions) to verify the useability of such a
prototype.

In this prototype, we will use experimental software developed in other projects.

This theme will be developed in cooperation with other internationa
teams, that have expressed interest. In particular, with INRIA-Sophia
Antipolis for the underlying software, and with the team "Medicis" og
the Ecole Polytechnique, that will be eventually the main team for the
possible running of the Journal.

The practical realization of the journal, with board of editors, call
for papers, support infrasctructure, etc, will be considered in a
second time, outside of the present project.



B1 Commutative algebra.

We will study characterizations of factoriality, extending the factorization algorithms from polynomial rings to UFD, non-nique factorization, irreducible decomposition of ideals.

Moreover, structure theorems and discussion of parametric systems.



B2 Real algebraic geometry

We will continue the study of the topological properties of the semialgebraic real varieties, in different directions:

* extend the algorithms already studied for surfaces to the non-generic case (including singularities), and to inequalities;

* extend the algorithms to dimension 3 and higher;

* complete the study and implementation of the real radical, and its applications.



C Algorithms for the computation of Groebner bases: extensions and implementations.



C1: Numerical and hybrid numerical-symbolic aspects.

We have recently demonstrated thatthe use of numerical and numerical-symbolic techniques for computational problems in computational commutative algebra is a viable alternative to the use of purely symbolic techniques, and is the only possibility when the initial equations are overeterminate.

We will continue the fruitful research in this field, on one side considering alternatives to Buchberger algorithm, on he other side studying the case of wild instability, and stabilization techniques based on syzygies and optimization methods.

C2: Implementation aspects

The algorithms will be studied in their practical realization, with extensions to the PoSSoLib. We will experiment new ideas based on the algorithms F4 and F5 of Faugere, and on the concept of involutive basis in a symbolic-numeric context, especially regarding aspects of numerical stability.

D: Applications

D1: Error correcting codes, cryptography

In error correcting codes the computational commutative algebra methods, Groebner bases in particular, have proved to be a powerful instrument to study the ECC properties, cyclic codes in particular. Fundamental conntributions have been found by our team.

Recently, it has been shown that several other codes, including the "algebraic geometric Goppa codes" and quasicyclic codes are cross sections of extended multicyclic codes. We will consider extending these successful cyclic code techniques to multicyclic codes, their extensions and cross-sections.

We'll study LDPC codes, to study with computational commutative algebra techniques their properties, that are presently just conjectured with probabilistic-heuristic techniques.

We'll study moreover incremental algorithms to decode cyclic codes with erasures, through the sudy of submodules. The extension to generalization of cyclic codes will be studies.

In cryptography, we'll study some aspects of cryptosystems connected with the solving of polynomial systems, and to cryptosystems using error correcting codes.

D2: CAGD

In Computer Aided Geometric design, (CAGD) several problems lead to systems of polynomial equations, mainly as (possibly approximate) implicitization and parametrization.

Another problem is the study of the topological properties of geometric entities defined mathematically, to verify the compatibility not only local of the models with the geometric properties conjectured for the described objects.

We'll study these problems, especially to optimize algorithms and implementations that are described in the other parts of the programme, and to get a set of significant examples.
